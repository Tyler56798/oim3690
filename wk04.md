# Week 04 Log

Date: 2026-06-12

## What I worked on

- MP1, start to finish: a website for a real client — Mike, a family friend in Houston who just left a 20-year HR career at Shell to start his own consultancy, Cashin Workforce Solutions LLC.
- Interviewed Mike on Monday (purpose, audience, services, style), sent him five reference sites from firms in his space (Korn Ferry, Mercer, Oliver Wyman, Slalom, Bain) along with the first deployed template, and had a feedback call Thursday.
- Made his requested changes: cut the services from four to three (Talent Acquisition, HR Operations, Employee Development), built out his About page from his real LinkedIn career history, and wired up a Calendly booking integration on the contact page.
- Deployed to GitHub Pages: [repo](https://github.com/Tyler56798/cashin-workforce-site) · [live site](https://tyler56798.github.io/cashin-workforce-site/)

## What I learned

- The client's taste is not my taste. I think the site is a bit basic and could use more work — Mike loved it exactly as-is and asked for zero design changes. The feedback call I expected to fill a page of revisions lasted minutes.
- Real content beats placeholder content by a mile. The About page went from bracketed filler ("[confirm employers and titles]") to a real career timeline once we pulled his actual LinkedIn history, and the page instantly felt legitimate.
- Don't guess at external facts. We almost could have used `calendly.com/mcashin` for his booking link — it turned out to belong to a completely different person (Mary Cashin). Now the Calendly URL is a single constant in the code, empty until Mike provides his real link.
- CSS I picked up this week: `clamp()` for fluid type sizes, CSS Grid with `minmax()` columns that collapse to one column on mobile, and `IntersectionObserver` for scroll-reveal animations with a `<noscript>` fallback.
- GitHub Pages vs Vercel: same static files, two deploy stories. Pages builds from the repo (and the URL lives under a subpath, so relative links matter); Vercel deploys from a folder push.

## AI interactions

### Interaction 1

- **What I asked**: After the feedback call, I gave Claude my notes from Mike — drop two services, add Employee Development, fill the About page from his LinkedIn, integrate Calendly.
- **What AI gave me**: All three changes, plus things I didn't ask for but needed: it caught that the word "fractional" still appeared in the Approach section after we dropped the Fractional HR service, and it replaced an invented "25+ years" stat with the verified "20+ years at Shell" from his profile.
- **What I kept / changed / rejected**: Kept nearly all of it. The important call I had to make myself was letting it use my logged-in LinkedIn session to read Mike's full profile — the public page hides his work history, so without that the bio would have stayed generic.

### Interaction 2

- **What I asked**: I pasted the whole MP1 assignment and asked what I was missing and how to get deployed by the deadline.
- **What AI gave me**: A gap list (no GitHub repo, no PROPOSAL.md/PRD.md, wrong deploy target — the site was on Vercel, the assignment requires GitHub Pages), then it scaffolded the repo, drafted the docs from our real project history, deployed to Pages, and ran Lighthouse (100 accessibility on all three pages).
- **What I kept / changed / rejected**: It first created the repo under the wrong GitHub account I had logged in — I had it redo everything under my school account. The layout sketch it could not do for me, correctly: that has to come from me by hand.
